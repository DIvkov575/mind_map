


Binomial Theorem v2, Newton
For $\alpha \in \mathbb{C}$  
$$(x+Y)^\alpha = \overset{\infty}{\underset{k = 0} {\Sigma}} \binom{n}{k} x^ky^{n-k}$$
whenever this makes sense

**Special cases**
i) $\alpha \in \mathbb{Z}$
ii) $x +y, y \in \mathbb{R}_+$
iii) for "formal powerseries"


--Pf--
Expand $f(x) = (x+y)^\alpha$
Using taylor's formula
$$f(x) = \overset{\infty}{\underset{k=0}{\Sigma}}\frac{f^{(k)}(0)}{k!}x^k$$
