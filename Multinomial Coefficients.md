Thm: For $\mathcal{A}  = \{ x_1, ... x_j \}$ for $n_1, \ldots, n_j \in \mathbb{N}$
such that $n = n_1 + \cdots + n_j$, there are 
$$ {{n!}\choose{n_1n_2 \ldots n_j}} = \frac{n!}{n_1!n_2!\cdots n_j!}$$
this can be interpreted as number of unique words from repeating letters
eg. APPLE 
$$\begin{align*}
s_1 = A, \quad n_1 = 1\\
s_2 = P, \quad n_2 = 2\\
s_3 = L, \quad n_3 = 1\\
s_4 = E, \quad n_4 = 1\\
\text{ans =} \frac{n!} {2!}
\end{align*}
